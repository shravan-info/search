India's 2025 Recap & Achivements

The year 2025 was an important year for India. During this year, India made strong progress in many fields such as science, technology, space, economy, sports, and international relations. The country achieved new records, launched successful missions, and gained respect at the global level. These achievements show India’s growth, innovation, and confidence as a developing nation.

Space & Science

  • SPADEX Mission Success : On January 16, 2025, ISRO successfully conducted the Space Docking Experiment (SPADEX) where two satellites docked in orbit.
  • First Indian at the ISS : Astronaut Shubhanshu Shukla became the first Indian to reach the International Space Station (ISS) as the part of joint mission.
  • ISRO's 100th Mission : ISRO celebrated its 100th space mission this year.

Sports Achivements

  • ICC Champions Trophy : The Indian Men's Cricket Team won the 2025 ICC Champions Trophy in Dubai, defeating New Zealand in the final.
  • Chess World Champion : Divya Deshmukh won the FIDE Women's Chess World Cup, making her the first Indian woman to achieve this title.
  • Kho-Kho World Cup : The Indian Women and Men both teams won the first ever Kho-Kho World Cup, defeating Nepal in the dominant performance.
  • Ice Hockey Milestone : The Indian Women's Ice Hockey team secured a landmark Bronze Medal at the Asia Cup.
  • Women's Cricket World Cup Victory : India won the ICC Women's Cricket World Cup 2025.
  • Asia Cup 2025 : India won the ACC Asia Cup 2025 defeating Pakistan.
  • World Para Athletics Championships : Hosted in New Delhi, India won 22 medals (6 Gold, 9 Silver, 7 Bronze)
  • Neeraj Chopra crossed 90m in javelin.

Economic & Trade

  • Fourth largest economy in the world (2025) : India surpassed Japan to become the world’s 4th largest economy.
  • Record rice reserves and export growth : Rice stocks surged to 57.57 million metric tons, supporting India’s role as top global exporter; exports up ~37%.
  • Infrastructure recognition : Build India Infra Awards 2025 celebrated major projects expanding highways, metros, airports, and logistics.
